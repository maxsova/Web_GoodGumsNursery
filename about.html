Our Mission and Vision

At Good Gums, we believe that every Australian garden deserves access to quality stock of native plant species. We are committed to cater for such demand in a sustainable and economical way.

Our History

Good Gums nursery was established in 1998. We have created great long-lasting connections with gardening community, biodiversity conservation groups, horticulturists and botanists in Victoria.

During the recent years of operation, we played an important part in the following programs:

  • Native plant seed bank.
    • Threatened Eucalyptus species of Australian Alps
    • Biodiversity sampling of the Little Desert
    • Grassy Woodland of Northern Victoria
    • Short-term seed bank
    • Seed propagation techniques, in collaboration with Deakin University
  • Revegetation projects
    • Glengower Reserve
    • Warden Lane creek reserve
    • Gardening with indigenous natives, Landcare initiative
    • National Tree Day, various locations
  • Community Gardening.
    • Glengower Reserve
    • Warden Lane creek reserve
    • Gardening with indigenous natives, Landcare initiative
    • National Tree Day, various locations
